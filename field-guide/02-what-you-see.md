# Field Guide 02 — Three new listening channels

Companion to Chapter 2, *What You See From the Other Side.*

The chapter argues that operators see things other leaders don't: a three-tier stack (model, app, harness), a small "Group 3" inside the larger AI-using population, and three sourced failure modes that show up wherever pilots fail to land. This exercise trains you to listen for each of those signals in your own meetings, your own news feed, and your own org.

Block twenty-five minutes. Bring last week's calendar.

## Listen 1 — The three-tier listen

Pick three pieces of AI commentary you've read or heard this week. For each one, ask:

- Is the speaker talking about the model, the app, or the harness?
- Does the speaker know there's a difference?

Most public AI commentary collapses all three into one. When you can name which tier a speaker is operating at, you stop arguing about the wrong thing. Write the three pieces down with the tier labeled.

## Listen 2 — The three-failure-mode recognition

Three failure patterns recur across organizations that try AI and fail to land it: prompt doom loop, pilot purgatory, silent failure. Read the chapter for definitions.

Walk through last week's calendar. For each AI-related meeting, decision, or update you sat in:

- Was anyone in that room describing one of these three patterns?
- Did the room name it, or did it pass without language?

The point isn't to diagnose colleagues. It's to notice how often the patterns appear unnamed.

## Listen 3 — The near-Group-3 identification

Group 3 is the small population inside Group 2 that's actually building. They're not on stage. They're inside your org, two layers down, on a Slack channel you're not in.

Name three people in your organization who fit Group 3. Write down what they've shipped that you only half know about. If you can't name three, that's the answer to a different question.

## After

Bring the three lists to your next 1:1 with a senior peer. The conversation that opens up is the one the chapter is trying to start.
