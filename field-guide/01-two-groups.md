# Field Guide 01 — The fifteen-minute self-audit

Companion to Chapter 1, *Two Groups Speaking Past Each Other.*

The chapter's argument is that the AI debate isn't a fight between factions. It's a gap between two populations: people who have built with agentic systems, and people who haven't. This exercise tells you which side you're on, and gives you the specific first action to cross.

Block fifteen minutes. Pen and paper, or a blank document.

## Section A — Last week, in your own work

Write the answer in one sentence each.

1. The last time AI did meaningful work for you, what was the work?
2. Where were you when it happened — chat window, IDE, terminal, somewhere else?
3. How long did the loop take, from prompt to usable output?
4. What did you do with the output? Did it ship, or did it land in a doc?

## Section B — Reading vs. building

Score each on a 0–3 scale.

- I read at least one AI-related piece per week. (0 / 1 / 2 / 3)
- I have personally directed an agent to do a multi-step task end-to-end. (0 / 1 / 2 / 3)
- I have written or modified a file that an agent reads to do its work. (0 / 1 / 2 / 3)
- I have shipped something this quarter that wouldn't have shipped without AI directing the build. (0 / 1 / 2 / 3)

## Section C — The honest read

If your Section A answers are all "chat window" and your Section B sums to under 4, you're in the gap. The book's claim is that the crossing is available to you in three months, six on the outside. The next chapter is the first half of the bridge.

If your Section B sums to 8 or higher, you're already on the other side. The rest of the book sharpens the practice and gives you language to bring others across.

## The first action

If you're in the gap and you read nothing else from this guide:

Install Claude Code today. Run it once on a real piece of your own work — not a tutorial repo, not a sandbox. The chapter you read next will have stronger purchase if your hands have touched the tool first.
