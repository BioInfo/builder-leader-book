# Field Guide 03 — Org assessment

Companion to Chapter 3, *Why Your Org Can't Cross Institutionally.*

The chapter's case is that institutions have specific structural reasons they can't cross the build gap on their own. Pilot-to-production ratios. The myth of AI literacy as the bottleneck. Centers of excellence that produce demos instead of operators. This exercise asks you to look at your own org through that lens.

Block thirty minutes. You'll need access to recent project data, or a colleague who has it.

## Part 1 — The pilot-to-production ratio

Count the AI pilots, demos, and proof-of-concepts your organization has run in the last twelve months. Then count the ones that are now in production, generating value, with a named owner.

- Pilots / POCs run: ___
- In production: ___
- Ratio: ___ : ___

Industry data puts the average around twelve-to-one. If yours is worse, that's a signal. If it's better, that's worth understanding — what did you do differently?

## Part 2 — The myth your org most deeply believes

The chapter names several institutional myths about why AI hasn't landed. Read the list, then circle the one your organization most deeply believes:

1. We need more AI literacy training first.
2. We need a center of excellence to lead.
3. We need governance to land before we can move.
4. We need the right vendor partner.
5. We need the data to be cleaner first.
6. We need leadership alignment first.

Each of these is a real concern. None of them is the bottleneck. The bottleneck is that no one with portfolio authority has personally crossed.

## Part 3 — One specific decision

Pick one AI-related decision your organization made in the last quarter. The kind that landed in a deck, got approved, and routed to a team to execute.

Now ask: how would that decision have been different if it had been made by a senior person who had personally directed a harness to build something for that exact problem?

Write the decision and the alternate version side by side. Not what you wish had happened. What an operator would have decided.

## After

The artifact this exercise produces — pilot ratio, myth identified, one re-decided decision — is the start of an honest conversation with your peer leadership group. The chapter argues you can't lead what you've never worn. This is the evidence.
